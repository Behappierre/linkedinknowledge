#### Tony Miles - Railway Journalist & Commentator
**Title:** Independent Photography Professional and Railway Journalist  
**Context:** PPM Five Minutes post comment  
**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/tonymilestrainspotter/

**Quote:**
> "The art of dispatch has been lost... on stations where there are still staff there's no incentive to 'get the train away' any more and this is certainly a cause of significant sub-threshold delays. I've lost count of the times I've been on a Liverpool-bound train at Manchester Piccadilly P14 where everyone is on board, signal is green, time to leave arrives... I look out and the dispatch staff are leaning on walls or pillars having a chat (or are yelling at people to 'stay behind the red line' in a manner that borders on rude rather than doing their primary task). 30 seconds late into the Castlefield Corridor is a disaster; TOC managers have pointed this out but no action is taken by NR."

**Why This Is Gold:**
- Specific operational example (Manchester Piccadilly P14)
- Dispatch discipline critique
- "30 seconds into Castlefield Corridor is a disaster" = cascade effect
- Network Rail accountability gap
- "Art of dispatch has been lost" = memorable framing

**Potential Content Angles:**
1. "The lost art of dispatch"
2. "When 30 seconds becomes a disaster: network cascade effects"
3. "Operational discipline vs. technology solutions"

**Status:** Not yet used in standalone post
