- **Latest success: Copenhagen Airport (28,303 impressions)**

## The Universal Formula (Present in ALL Top 7)

### 1. Opening Hook (First 2 Lines)
**Borrowed Credibility Quote:**
- "A captain panics if he's going to miss his slot" (Copenhagen - 28K)
- "A missed charge at 2pm becomes a service failure at 6pm" (Electric Bus - 23K)
- "The sensors detected movement 4 hours before" (Shap - 37K)

**OR Personal Experience:**
- "We've missed our slot. The next available slot is in two and a half hours" (Copenhagen)
- "Currently on a Pendolino to Glasgow..." (Train Display)

---

### 2. The "I Learned" Frame (Lines 3-5)
**Template:**
```
At the time, I didn't understand [the mechanics/the reason/why].

Then [I visited / I learned / I saw] [the insight].

[Operator quote capturing the lesson]
```

**Why This Works:**
- Shows intellectual humility
- Creates relatability
- Demonstrates listening to practitioners
- Makes you interpreter, not expert

---

### 3. Named Concept (Middle Section)
**Examples:**
- "Best Planned, First Served" (Copenhagen)
- "The Correlation Gap" / "The Deployment Gap" (Shap)
- "The Paraphrase Penalty" (Groundhog Day)
- "PPM at Five Minutes" (PPM)

**Why This Works:**
- Creates professional vocabulary
- Easy to reference in work discussions
- Builds thought leadership IP
- Demonstrates frameworks thinking

---

### 4. Concrete Operational Details
**Requirements:**
- Specific numbers, systems, scenarios
- Technical terminology (proves insider knowledge)
- Real examples, not abstract concepts
- Cascading impacts demonstrated

**Examples:**
- "4 hours before, 10mm threshold, 04:30 timing" (Shap)
- "Pantograph chargers, wrong bay, cold days" (Electric Bus)
- "Keep your plan, keep your priority. Miss your slot? Back of the queue" (Copenhagen)

---

### 5. Orchestration Insight
**Key Phrases:**
- "That's the gap between Control and Orchestration" (Copenhagen)
- "Monitoring systems need a platform where they're operational from day one" (Shap)
- "Not smarter algorithms, smarter coordination" (Electric Bus)

**Pattern:**
- Not technology alone
- Human + system coordination
- Makes complexity invisible
- Platform thinking (Netcompany positioning)

---

### 6. Professional Visual
**Types:**
- **Real photos** (Train Display - stops scrolling)
- **Split-screen comparisons** (Copenhagen - makes connection immediate)
- **Technical diagrams** (Shap - shows expertise)
- **Metaphor images** (Electric Bus - memorable)

**Requirements:**
- High quality, not amateur
- Mobile-readable
- Clear subject/metaphor
- Supports your point

---

### 7. Strategic Engagement Question
**Examples:**
- "What would 'Best Planned, First Served' look like for rail?" (Copenhagen)
- "How quickly do your monitoring investments become operationally useful?" (Shap)
- "Does any other sector have such an extreme Peak/Average ratio?" (48 Hours)

**Pattern:**
- Open-ended, sector-specific
- Invites comparison or application
- No obvious answer (creates debate)
- Positions you as facilitator

## What NOT to Do

### Red Flags (Stop and Rewrite)

**Opening:**
- ❌ "Proud to have represented..."
- ❌ "Delighted to announce..."
- ❌ "I recently attended..."

**Content:**
- ❌ No specific numbers (all abstract)
- ❌ No named people (generic "stakeholders")
- ❌ Bullet points (looks like PowerPoint)
- ❌ Buzzwords without explanation
- ❌ No engagement question

**Tone:**
- ❌ Sounds like press release
- ❌ Selling/promotional
- ❌ Overly formal/academic
- ❌ Jargon-heavy without explanation
