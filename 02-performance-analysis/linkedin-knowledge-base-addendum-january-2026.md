## Writing Rules (ABSOLUTE)

### Never Use Em Dashes (—)
**Rule:** Do not use the em dash character (—) anywhere in LinkedIn posts. Ever.

**Alternatives:**
- Use a comma instead: "politicians shouldn't run railways, experts should"
- Use a colon instead: "one goal: better passenger experience"
- Use a full stop and new sentence: "This layer is moving. The question is what comes next."
- Use a hyphen for compound adjectives: "track-train governance"
- Rephrase to avoid the pause entirely

## Critical Lessons (Cumulative)

### Never Qualify Borrowed Credibility (ABSOLUTE — confirmed repeatedly)

**Rule:** Direct quotes only. If you cannot quote directly, anonymize completely without announcing it. If the quote's original context differs from your post's context, bridge it explicitly rather than hiding the shift.

---

### RAIB Named Framework Pattern — Third Confirmation (Post 22)

Shap ("Correlation Gap", "Deployment Gap"), now Helpston ("Override Drift"). Each post coins a transferable organisational pattern from a RAIB report.

**Rule:** Read the RAIB report, identify the systemic failure, name the mechanism as an observable organisational pattern, define it clinically, connect to the assurance or coordination layer. The framework must be transferable beyond rail.

---

### Named Frameworks Can Enter Professional Practice Discourse Within 24 Hours (NEW — Post 22)

Allan Spence cited Override Drift at the Global Level Crossing Network meeting the day after posting.

**Rule:** A named framework that is genuinely useful will be picked up by practitioners within 24-48 hours. Create the conditions — clinical definition, RAIB credibility, transferability — and do not control the uptake.

---

### Named Framework Posts Continue Growing at 10-15 Days (NEW — Post 22 trajectory)

Helpston: 37,692 at 2 days, 50,484 at 10 days, 51,084 at 15 days.

**Rule:** For RAIB framework posts, use 10 days as the minimum assessment window.

---

### Without a Named Framework, RAIB Posts Fall Short — Confirmed by Audenshaw (Post 19a)

**Rule:** A RAIB post without a named framework tops out at the Good tier floor. The Audenshaw interface failure pattern deserves its own named framework in a future post. Candidate names: "Interface Blindspot", "Split Custody Gap", "Discipline Fragmentation".

### Named Target Organisations Appear in Their Own Post's Demographics — ABM Signal (NEW — Post 25)

TfL at 5% for a TfL Commissioner quote. NR also at 5% on the same post.

**Rule:** Check whether the quoted organisation appears in the top companies demographics. Its presence confirms the content reached the target room.

### Cross-Sector Content Bridges to Rail When Orchestration Angle Is Explicit (NEW — Post 25)

Rail Transportation at 7% on an electric bus post.

**Rule:** Rail professionals engage with bus, aviation, or infrastructure content when it is framed as a coordination/systems problem rather than a sector-specific operations problem.

---

### Highlighted Document Screenshots Are Low-Contrast Visuals in the LinkedIn Feed (NEW — Post 26)

The green highlight on white document paper blended with LinkedIn's grey interface. Dense surrounding text diluted the scroll-stop impact despite the intellectual interest of the image.

**Rule:** For document-sourced posts, the cover page is the safer visual default unless contrast is demonstrably high. A close crop of a single highlighted sentence on plain background outperforms a full-page document screenshot. The visual must stop a scroll; proving close reading is secondary.

---
