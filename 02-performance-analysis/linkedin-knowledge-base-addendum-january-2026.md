## Writing Rules (ABSOLUTE)

### Never Use Em Dashes (—)
**Rule:** Do not use the em dash character (—) anywhere in LinkedIn posts. Ever.

**Alternatives:**
- Use a comma instead: "politicians shouldn't run railways, experts should"
- Use a colon instead: "one goal: better passenger experience"
- Use a full stop and new sentence: "This layer is moving. The question is what comes next."
- Use a hyphen for compound adjectives: "track-train governance"
- Rephrase to avoid the pause entirely

## Critical Lessons (Cumulative)

### Never Qualify Borrowed Credibility (ABSOLUTE — confirmed repeatedly)

**Rule:** Direct quotes only. If you cannot quote directly, anonymize completely without announcing it. If the quote's original context differs from your post's context, bridge it explicitly rather than hiding the shift.

---

### Seniority Formula Now Expanded — CXO, Partner, Owner Included (RULE CHANGE — April 2026)

LinkedIn now provides a more granular seniority breakdown. The formula has been expanded to: Senior % + Director % + VP % + CXO % + Partner % + Owner %.

Post 27 (Bee Network) is the first post documented under the new formula: 73% expanded vs 64% old formula. Historical posts scored under old formula should be interpreted as understating seniority quality by 7-12 percentage points.

**Rule:** Always use the expanded formula for all posts from April 2026 onwards. Benchmarks in SKILL.md updated accordingly.

---

### Scenario Hooks Without a Named Source Need Geographic or Institutional Tagging to Reach ABM Targets (NEW — Post 27)

Post 27 opened with "A bus runs four minutes late. The operator knows. The authority finds out at month end." — a strong scenario hook. Manchester appeared at only 2% despite the post being explicitly about the Bee Network.

**Rule:** A scenario hook without a named source, organisation, or tagged contact gives the algorithm no geographic or institutional seed. To reach a specific target geography (e.g. Greater Manchester), either name a contact from that authority in the body text or quote a practitioner from that organisation.

---

### A Photo Credit Self-Comment Has No Analytical Value (NEW — Post 27)

Post 27's only comment was "Photo: William McCue / Unsplash" — 62 impressions, no engagement momentum.

**Rule:** If photo attribution is required for a stock image, place it at the end of the post text, not as a self-comment. A self-comment slot should be reserved for substantive content that extends the argument, adds a data point, or opens a follow-up question. Wasting the self-comment slot on a photo credit forfeits the algorithmic second-wave distribution opportunity.

---

### RAIB Named Framework Pattern — Third Confirmation (Post 22)

**Rule:** Read the RAIB report, identify the systemic failure, name the mechanism as an observable organisational pattern, define it clinically, connect to the assurance or coordination layer. The framework must be transferable beyond rail.

---

Allan Spence cited Override Drift at the Global Level Crossing Network meeting the day after posting.

**Rule:** A named framework that is genuinely useful will be picked up by practitioners within 24-48 hours. Create the conditions — clinical definition, RAIB credibility, transferability — and do not control the uptake.
