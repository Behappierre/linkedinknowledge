**The airspace analogy is exceptionally strong:**

- Triggers the reaction: "Of course - so why does this happen?"

### 2. Three-Layer Framework = Intellectual Structure

Previous posts used: one insight, one problem, one orchestration angle.

This post introduced **a named three-layer model:**
- Layer 1: Institutional (legislation, ownership)
- Layer 2: Operational (track-train governance)

**Why frameworks work better than single insights:**
- Readers remember the structure
- They can disagree with one layer while accepting others
- It invites extension (Steve White added his own five-layer model)
- It positions you as a systems thinker, not just an observer
- Leon Kong immediately engaged because he works on exactly Layer 3

This post achieved something new: **three distinct borrowed credibility sources at different hierarchy levels:**
- Government Minister (Heidi Alexander) - policy authority
- Infrastructure leader (Alex Hynes) - operational authority  
- Train operator MD (Steve White) - delivery authority

The layering creates a complete picture. Reader can't dismiss the post by questioning one source.

### 4. Personal Stakes Signal ("as I prepare to speak on a panel in Scotland")

## New Strategic Learnings

### Learning 1: Minister-Level Borrowed Credibility Unlocks Policy Audience

The Heidi Alexander quote reached:
- DfT directly (1.1%)
- Rail Transportation professionals (10.1%)
- Broader policy/consulting community (5.6%)

**Rule:** When content involves government or policy, opening with the most senior government voice available maximises reach into the policy community.

---

### Learning 2: Multi-Source Borrowed Credibility Creates Richer Authority

Previous posts: one quote, one source.
This post: three quotes, three authority levels (Minister > Infrastructure Chief > Operator MD).

**The layered effect:**
- Can't be dismissed by questioning one source
- Covers institutional + operational + delivery perspectives
- Steve White extended it with a FIFTH layer model (showing the framework inspired response)

**Template for future policy/reform posts:** Minister or regulator quote + infrastructure leader quote + operator quote = complete authority stack.

---

### Learning 3: Named Frameworks Invite Extension and Debate

The "Three Layers" model:
- Steve White responded with his own five-layer model
- Leon Kong engaged on Layer 3 specifically ("just walk into any ROC")
- Peter Coleman challenged the missing incentive layer

**Named frameworks generate richer comments than observations.** When you give people a structure to agree with, disagree with, or extend, they engage more deeply.

**Comparison:**
- "Here's a problem I observed" → 3-5 comments agreeing
- "Here's a framework for thinking about the problem" → 10+ comments extending, challenging, enriching
