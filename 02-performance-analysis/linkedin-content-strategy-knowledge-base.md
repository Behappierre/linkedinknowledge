## Writing Principles

### Opening Hooks (Critical - First 2 Lines)

**GOOD Opening Patterns:**
1. **Dialogue:** "'Tuesdays and Thursdays.' That was the answer when I asked..."
2. **Shocking stat:** "£355 million. That's what Low Adhesion costs..."
3. **Real-time scene:** "Currently on a Pendolino to Glasgow, the video screen says..."
4. **Regulatory finding:** "The sensors detected movement 4 hours before the Shap landslip. But it was below the 10mm threshold..."
5. **Provocative question:** "Is this a fail-fast experiment, or a must-deliver benefit?"
6. **CTO quote:** "In a meeting last week, a CTO stopped us mid-presentation..."
7. **Operator insight:** "A missed charge at 2pm becomes a service failure at 6pm."

**BAD Opening Patterns:**
1. ✗ "Proud to have represented Netcompany at..." (corporate HR-speak)
2. ✗ "Investing in X can enhance Y. However..." (passive, academic)
3. ✗ "Fascinating discussion with..." (consultant preamble)
4. ✗ "I was honored to attend..." (humble-brag)
5. ✗ "We are recruiting, if you want to know more..." (off-brand)

**Rule:** Your opening must grab attention in 2 seconds of scrolling. Lead with the insight, not the context.

---

### Structure That Works

**The Proven Formula:**
1. **Hook** (shocking stat, dialogue, real-time problem, regulatory finding)
2. **Context** (why this matters, who said it, news relevance)
3. **Concrete details** (specific numbers, routes, companies, percentages)
4. **The orchestration insight** (how coordination solves it)
5. **Proof or validation** (operator comment, engineer feedback, real output)
6. **Engagement hook** (question to audience)

**Example (Shap Landslip post):**
1. Hook: "The sensors detected movement 4 hours before... But it was below the 10mm threshold"
2. Context: RAIB urgent safety advice, no serious injuries, media coverage
3. Concrete: 04:30 timing, "tipped over and subsumed", months between installation/integration
