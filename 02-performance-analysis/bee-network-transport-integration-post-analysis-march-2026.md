A bus runs four minutes late. The operator knows. The authority that commissioned the service finds out at month end.

By then, the route has run 3,000 more times.

That is the real challenge inside transport integration. Not just who holds the contract, but who sees the network clearly enough, early enough, to act.

Greater Manchester completed bus franchising in January 2025, the first city outside London to bring buses back under local control in 40 years. It is an ambitious and important shift: multiple operators, one authority, one passenger promise.

But the system does not stop there.

The Metrolink franchise, a contract worth up to £1.6bn over eleven years, is already in procurement. Commuter rail joins the Bee Network from December 2026. As Great British Railways takes shape, another layer of accountability is forming across parts of the same passenger journey.

Each step makes strategic sense. But each one also adds another operator, another contract, and another performance dataset, before the previous layer has been properly embedded.

And that is before the modes outside formal franchising. E-scooters, shared bikes, demand-responsive transport. The last-mile links that often determine whether the journey works at all. Their own data, their own operational picture, and little real-time relationship with the rest of the system.

That creates a structural tension contracts alone cannot solve.

Each operator measures performance from its own data. The authority reconciles it retrospectively. The passenger experiences one journey.

A shared real-time operational picture is not a reporting upgrade. It is the coordination layer that makes integrated transport actually work.

The question is not only who holds each contract. It is who is accountable for the journey between them.

## 12. Lessons Learned

**Scenario hooks without a named source need geographic or institutional tagging to reach ABM targets.** The "a bus runs four minutes late" opening is effective as a hook but gives the algorithm no signal about who should see this post. A named contact from TfGM or GMCA in the body text, or the specific routes/network named earlier in the post, would have seeded geographic distribution to Manchester.

**A photo credit self-comment has no analytical value and should be absorbed into the post body.** If photo attribution is required, place it at the end of the post text ("Photo: [credit]") rather than posting a self-comment. A self-comment that only attributes a photo wastes the algorithmic second-wave distribution opportunity.

**Multi-modal franchising complexity is an underexplored content thread.** The post's structural argument (contracts layer accountability without solving coordination) is not fully developed in the series. A follow-up post with a named TfGM or Bee Network practitioner quote could reach the regional commissioning audience that this post missed.

**The expanded seniority formula confirms the quality of this audience even at modest impression scale.** 73% under the new formula means the 1,244 impressions were concentrated in the decision-maker layer. Distribution was the constraint, not targeting.
