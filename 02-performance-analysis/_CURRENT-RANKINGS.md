## 🚨 Structural Failure Patterns (Proven With Data)

### 1. The Paraphrase Penalty (26x reduction)
Clean borrowed credibility posts average ~35,000 impressions.
"Not the exact word but I paraphrase" (Groundhog Day): **1,088 impressions at posting** (grew to 1,870 — delayed but still penalised).

### 2. The Bullet Point Penalty (26x reduction)
Shap Landslip (prose, RAIB topic): **37,765 impressions**.
Audenshaw (bullets, RAIB topic): **1,539 impressions**.

### 3. The Same-Day Posting Penalty
EU Telematics + Heathrow posted same day (Feb 24): both capped at ~6-7K.
Estimated combined loss: 20K-25K impressions.
**Rule: Never post twice on the same day by choice.**

### 4. The Sunday Posting Ceiling
Underground/Solar (Sunday 8:31am): **953 impressions** despite 64.1% seniority.
The post had a real news hook and quality audience but no distribution window.
**Rule: Never post on Sunday.**

### 5. The Friday Ceiling
Scotland/Bill Reeve (Friday): **3,218 impressions** — strong content but ceiling confirmed.
DfT Transport Data Action Plan (Friday 7:35am): 526 at 10 hours, projected 800-1,500.
**Rule: Never post precision content on a Friday. Exception: time-sensitive policy documents where timeliness outweighs ceiling risk.**

### 6. Corporate Announcement Ceiling
Posts with "Today, [Company] announced" framing cap at 6K-10K regardless of content quality.
**Rule: Strip corporate framing. Frame as operational insight instead.**

### 7. The Highlighted Document Screenshot Trap (NEW — Post 26)
Green highlight on white document text blends into LinkedIn's grey feed interface.
Dense surrounding text provides insufficient scroll-stop contrast.
**Rule: Use the cover page for policy document posts, or crop to a single high-contrast highlighted sentence. Proof of close reading is secondary to stopping a scroll.**
