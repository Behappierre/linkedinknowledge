#### The Post

> "The Rail Minister should no more be making timetabling decisions than I should be coordinating airspace above Heathrow."
>
> Heidi Alexander, Minister of State for Transport, said this at last week's George Bradshaw Address. A refreshing admission: ministers should set direction and accountability, while railway professionals run operations. But as I prepare to speak on a panel in Scotland next month, I keep asking a harder question. Who, or what, is actually running the experience for the passenger?
>
> I keep returning to three layers that need to move together.
>
> The first is institutional: legislation, ownership, accountability. The Railways Bill is live, eight of fourteen DfT operators are in public ownership, Shadow GBR is operating. This layer is moving.
>
> The second is operational: integrated track-train governance, joint objectives, shared performance culture. Scotland is ahead here, with the ScotRail-Network Rail Alliance formalised since October 2024. Alex Hynes said it directly at the Bradshaw Address: "The quicker we start acting as one railway, the quicker we can start delivering the changes passengers really want."
>
> The third layer is digital, and it is the one that actually talks to the passenger. It is the real-time data that ensures the app matches the platform screen. The disruption planning that routes you around a problem before you have noticed it. The ticketing that simply works. This layer is where Layers 1 and 2 either materialise for the person on the platform, or quietly stall behind office walls.
>
> Steve White, MD of South Eastern Railway, put the requirement well: "GBR needs to work bottom up as well as top down, fuelled by whole systems thinking and collaboration." For a passenger, the system is not a piece of legislation. It is the information in their hand and the train on the track.
>
> Scotland has the governance conditions. The question now is whether the digital layer is being designed for the person on the platform, or arriving later as an afterthought.
>
> For those working in rail reform: where does the digital layer sit in your programme right now?
>
> #GBR #RailReform #SystemsOrchestration #UKRail
