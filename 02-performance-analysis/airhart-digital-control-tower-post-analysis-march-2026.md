## 8. Post Development Notes

**Dual-purpose structure risk:** This post makes two simultaneous arguments - aviation proof point (Heathrow AIRHART) and rail prescription (GBR Digital Control Tower). The risk identified in real-time analysis was that readers must follow both threads. The self-comments help by separating the two clearly, but the main post body carries both.

**Monday posting:** Outside the optimal Tuesday-Thursday window. Likely costs 15-25% of potential impressions in the first distribution wave. The 1,833 at 8 hours is strong enough to suggest this is not severely penalised.

**Links in comments, not body:** Correct execution. Both Rail Magazine and Global Railway Review links housed in self-comments. No external links in main post.

**Echave quote reuse:** The Javier Echave quote was first used in Post 19 (Heathrow Announcement). Reusing it here is acceptable because: (a) the framing is different (Post 19 was announcement framing; this post is proof-of-concept framing), and (b) 6 days separation means most of the audience will see it fresh.

## 12. Lessons Learned

**The self-comment hook is stronger than the post opener.** "A 12-minute signal failure cascades into a three-hour, multi-million pound disruption" is visceral and specific in a way the Echave quote is not. Future posts on this theme should consider leading with the failure scenario rather than the solution quote.

**Aviation contacts engage authentically.** Carl Soutra reacted and connected without prompting. The post reached him because it named his employer (Heathrow) and spoke to his operational experience. This confirms the account-based pattern: name the organisation, reach the people inside it.

**Dual publication amplification works but requires careful self-comment structure.** Separating the two links into distinct self-comments with distinct frames (operational fragmentation vs. decarbonisation) means readers can follow either thread without confusion. This is the correct execution pattern for multi-publication posts.
