## The Post (Final Version)

> "These are not technology problems. These are ultimately integration problems."
>
> Daryn McCombe, Train Service Delivery and Performance Director at GWR, said this at #RIC26 this morning. Not as a slide headline. After describing two very vivid operational scenarios from the last 12 months.
>
> In the first, three severe storms hit Wales in quick succession. Each time, GWR and NR engineers had to physically travel to check structures, track, power and monitoring systems, because the data hadn't been brought together in a way that could be interpreted without being there. Passengers and media kept asking the same question: why can't you run trains? The answer wasn't engineering. It was an incomplete picture.
>
> In the second, sequential trespasser and infrastructure incidents across the Western route meant every recovery option from the first event was spent before the second resolved. GWR lost around 30 percent of trains across the day. His point: if the full operating picture had been visible faster, recovery would have looked different.
>
> His vision for what comes next is not a better tool. It is one integrated view combining track, train, crew, fleet, weather and events, giving operators, controllers, maintainers and planners the clarity and foresight to make decisions, not more screens to stare at.
>
> he was describing what I usually call operational orchestration.
>
> The shift he is asking the supply chain for is a different way of thinking about the ecosystem. Build for the whole system, not the individual asset.
>
> If integration is the real constraint, who in your organisation owns that layer? And are they designing it with the operators in the room?
>
> #RailTransform #GBR #SystemsOrchestration #UKRail
